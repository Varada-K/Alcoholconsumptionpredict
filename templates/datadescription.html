Data Description


The prediction algorithm is trained on an open-source dataset obtained from Kaggle, titled "Smoking and Drinking Dataset with Body Signal".


The data is free to use for non-commercial purpose.








Click each button to know more about the dataset:


The dataset containing body signal data related to smoking and alcohol consumption is a valuable resource for predictive analysis. It originates from the National Health Insurance Service in Korea and has undergone meticulous curation to safeguard privacy and data security by excluding any personal or sensitive information.

Analysis of Body Signals: The dataset contains a wide range of body signal measurements, incorporating diverse physiological and health-related characteristics. Researchers and data scientists can utilize this extensive dataset to explore the connections between these signals and the patterns of smoking and drinking behaviors, deriving valuable insights from the data.
Classification of Smokers and Drinkers: A prominent use case for this dataset involves categorizing individuals into two specific groups: smokers and drinkers. Through the utilization of machine learning and statistical methodologies, professionals can create predictive models capable of accurately determining if an individual belongs to either or both of these groups using their body signal data.

Within the Smoking and Drinking Dataset, a diverse set of parameters is present, incorporating factors like age, height, weight, blood pressure, cholesterol levels, among others. These parameters serve as fundamental components for comprehending the physiological traits within the dataset. Moreover, the dataset comprises details concerning smoking behaviors, alcohol consumption patterns, and a spectrum of body signal measurements.
